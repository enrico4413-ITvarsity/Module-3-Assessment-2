Paragon of steak perfection

Filet Mignon's popularity extends far beyond the borders of France, where it was first named. It's a staple on fine dining menus around the world, cherished for its exceptional quality and exquisite dining experience.

Ingredients:

The preparation of Filet Mignon is a testament to culinary craftsmanship. The steak is often cut into thick, round medallions, which are then seasoned with salt and pepper. It is typically cooked by grilling, pan-searing, or broiling to achieve a delicate crust on the outside while maintaining its tender interior. The goal is to sear the exterior quickly to lock in the juices and retain the tenderness of the meat.
